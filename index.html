Floods will affect twice the world's population by the end of the decade

Myriam Vidal Valero

Researchers used satellites to map floods in the world and found they have been increasing due to climate change. They estimate that by 2030, 147 million will be hit by floods worldwide.

Oct. 14, 2021

Hurricane Ida killed at least 25 people in the New York area. Photo: Matt Rourke.

On September 1, 2021, hurricane Ida hit New York City, causing unprecedented floods. At least 21 people were killed. Flooding affects more people than any other environmental hazard in the world, causing house displacement, economic losses, and a rise in exposure to disease. In 57 countries,the increase in flood exposure is expected to outpace future population growth, especially in Asia and Africa.

As global warming is increasing the risk of floods happening across the world, countries need to address the issue by creating more effective preventive and emergency response plans, and by addressing climate change altogether.

Flood Affects

From 2000 to 2015 the population affected by floods grew from 58 million to 86 million. Satellite monitoring projections forecast that the population exposed to floods will increase further for 2030.

People displaced

In the U.S., approximately 45,523 people are yearly displaced from their homes by floods. From 2008 to 2018, floods caused over half of climate and weather-related displacements in the world.

Fatalities

Flooding is one of the main weather-related disasters that cause numerous fatalities every year across the world. In the U.S., approximately 100 people are killed annually by floods.

Driving or walking into floodwaters accounts for more than 86% of total flood fatalities.

Economic loss

An estimated $651 billion in flood damages occurred globally from 2000 to 2019. Yet, only 13% of disaster funds are allocated to preparedness, mitigation and adaptation.
